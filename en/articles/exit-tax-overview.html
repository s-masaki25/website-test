Exit Tax Overview

Japan's exit tax system requires careful planning for individuals with significant assets who are considering relocation abroad.

This article provides an overview of Japan's exit tax (kokugai tenshutsu-ji kazei seido) and key considerations for affected individuals.

What is Exit Tax?

Japan's exit tax, introduced in 2015, applies to individuals who have held tax residency in Japan for at least 5 of the past 10 years and own financial assets exceeding 100 million yen at the time of departure.

Key Considerations

Understanding the scope and timing of exit tax obligations is essential for effective tax planning. Consult with qualified tax professionals before making relocation decisions.
